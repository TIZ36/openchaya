# Chaya — Design Context

## Users

Chaya's users are **general users**, not developers or power-users — though the product's surface area (agents, MCP servers, skills, knowledge base, topology) is genuinely deep. The interface has to carry that depth without intimidating.

**Context of use**: desktop-first (Electron), with sessions that range from a quick "ask an agent something" check-in to long-form configuration and exploration. Users are often sitting with it for a while. Focus matters; noise distracts.

**Core job**: have conversations with AI agents the user has shaped, and feel ownership over what those agents are and do. Not "chat with a bot" — "keep company with something I made."

## Brand Personality

**Three words: calm, impressed, handmade.**

- **Calm** — the interface lowers the user's heart rate. Generous spacing, quiet palette, no glow, no urgency. Motion exists but is unhurried. Nothing demands attention unless it earned it.
- **Impressed** — both senses. (1) Leaves a lasting impression: one or two details a user remembers and tells a friend about. (2) Impressed in the physical sense — letterpress, deboss, ink soaking into cotton paper, a hand-set type specimen. Tactile, pressed, slightly uneven.
- **Handmade** — subtly imperfect, human. Not the plastic sterility of most AI product UIs. Feels like it was *made*, not assembled from a component library. Asymmetries are intentional. The occasional hand-drawn mark, the slightly-wrong-on-purpose rule, the unexpected ornament.

Emotional goal: the feeling of opening a well-made notebook, not the feeling of opening a SaaS dashboard.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Theme**: **Light mode default**, dark mode available. Light fits "calm + handmade" (paper, ink, daylight). Dark is for long evening sessions.

**Visual metaphor**: *a letterpress notebook*. Cream/ivory paper, deep aubergine ink, hand-set type. Space is generous. Rules are drawn, not always rendered. Things have weight — when a message lands it feels pressed onto the page.

**Palette** (OKLCH, perceptually tuned):
- **Paper** (background): warm off-white, faintly tinted toward accent — `oklch(0.985 0.004 310)`
- **Page** (card/surface): slightly lifted — `oklch(0.975 0.005 310)`
- **Ink** (primary text): deep plum-black, never pure black — `oklch(0.18 0.02 310)`
- **Pencil** (secondary text): muted warm gray — `oklch(0.48 0.01 310)`
- **Rule** (borders): `oklch(0.90 0.008 310)`
- **Accent** (aubergine / fountain-pen ink): `oklch(0.38 0.12 310)` — dialed-down from the existing purple, warmer, more ink-like. Used sparingly (10% rule).
- **Marginalia** (warm highlight, like pencil underline): `oklch(0.92 0.04 75)` — a soft ochre, for hover states and gentle emphasis.
- **Dark-mode equivalents**: the same hues flipped — deep aubergine paper, cream ink.

**Killed**:
- `#00ff88` neon green (crypto/GMGN holdover — breaks calm)
- `bg-gradient-rainbow`, `bg-gradient-primary`, all decorative gradients
- All `glow-*` shadows (neon glows are the opposite of calm)
- `bounce-in` animation and elastic easing (dated, tacky)
- Pure black (`#000`) and pure white (`#fff`) anywhere in the UI

**Typography** (must move off system defaults):
- **Display**: a warm, slightly narrow serif with real personality — candidates: *Söhne Breit*, *Tiempos*, *Canela*, *GT Alpina*, or on Google Fonts: *Gelasio*, *Literata*, *Young Serif*. Avoid Fraunces, Newsreader, Lora, Crimson, Playfair, DM Serif (all banned by the skill).
- **Body**: a humanist sans with a hand-cut feel. Candidates: *Söhne*, *GT America*, or free: *Public Sans*, *Commissioner*, *Figtree* (not DM Sans, not Inter, not Plus Jakarta).
- **Mono** (for code/API): *JetBrains Mono* or *Berkeley Mono* — not IBM Plex Mono, not Space Mono.
- Vary weight more than size. Five-step scale, 1.25 ratio minimum. Cap body line length at 68ch.

**Motion**:
- Default duration 180–240ms
- Easing: `cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)` (ease-out-quart) — never bounce, never elastic
- Only animate `transform` and `opacity`
- Page/content entries: subtle fade + 4–6px rise, never slide from far away
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`

## Design Principles

1. **Paper, not screen.** Treat the canvas as something that receives ink. Surfaces have weight and slight warmth. Pure white is forbidden; pure black is forbidden. Everything tints toward the accent hue.

2. **Sparing accent.** The aubergine appears *rarely* — on the one thing the user should look at. If two things are accented on a screen, one of them is wrong. Rely on typography and spacing for hierarchy, not color.

3. **Handmade imperfection.** Introduce small, deliberate human touches: a hand-drawn rule instead of a `<hr>`, an asymmetric card layout where a symmetric one would be safer, a marginal note in a serif italic. These are earned, not decorative.

4. **Generous, rhythmic space.** Vary spacing for hierarchy. A heading breathes; a dense list compacts. Never use the same padding everywhere. 4pt scale with semantic tokens.

5. **Quiet motion.** Motion confirms, it never performs. No stagger-cascades, no spring bounces, no attention-grabbing entrances. A message settles onto the page; it doesn't arrive.

6. **Depth without drama.** No glassmorphism, no glow, no heavy drop shadows. Depth comes from one-pixel rules, subtle surface tint shifts, and the occasional letterpress-style inset shadow.
